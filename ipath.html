Institute for Psychedelic-Assisted Therapies & Healing (IPATH) Website Redesign

Process: I led the UX/UI design process to create an engaging, user-friendly, and informative website that builds trust and supports client needs. My approach involved conducting competitive analysis, empathy mapping, and usability testing to ensure the design effectively communicated complex therapy concepts in an accessible way.

Skills/Techniques: UX research, empathy mapping, information architecture, user flows, wireframing, prototyping, usability testing, and visual design using Figma, Canva, and Whimsical.

My Process

Research

In the Research stage, I reviewed a variety of websites for psychedelic therapy clinics and related mental health services to understand how they present information, engage users, and establish trust. I analyzed their approaches to design, content layout, and key features like appointment booking and safety protocols. This helped me identify common practices, as well as opportunities for IPATH to stand out with a user-centered and visually distinctive approach.

Ideate

In the Ideate stage, I created detailed user flows to map out the key interactions users would have with the website, from exploring therapy options to booking appointments. This process ensured a seamless and intuitive experience tailored to user needs.

Wireframes

I was able to develop mid-fi and hi-fi wireframes using Figma.

Design

In the design stage, I translated insights into a visually appealing and user-friendly website. By focusing on a calming aesthetic, intuitive navigation, and accessible content, I ensured the design reflected IPATH’s values and supported user needs seamlessly.

Prototypes

In the prototype stage, I gathered user feedback to evaluate the website’s functionality, usability, and design. This process helped identify areas for improvement and ensured the final product met user expectations effectively.

Conclusion

This project reinforced the importance of balancing user needs with technical feasibility. I learned that even small design elements, like confirmation pop-ups and intuitive navigation, can significantly enhance the user experience. Moving forward, I’m excited to apply these insights to future projects to create impactful, user-centered designs.
